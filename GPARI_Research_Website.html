01 | Abstract

What This Research Is About

Thousands of adolescents die each year not from substances but from danger itself. I trained a gradient boosting classifier on Add Health Wave IV (n = 6,504) to test whether COMT Val158Met drives compulsive risk-taking through inhibitory control failure rather than reward insensitivity, which is the DRD2 mechanism. The model achieved AUC = 0.885. Causal mediation showed inhibitory control failure carries 67% of the total indirect COMT effect (95% CI: 54% to 79%). I built the GPARI to turn that finding into something usable: a free screening tool that runs on any device, takes nine questionnaire responses, and tells a counselor which genetic pathway is driving a student's risk.
02 | Introduction

Why I Built This

In 2022, unintentional injury was the leading cause of death for Americans aged 1 to 44 (CDC, 2022). A lot of those deaths came from compulsive risk-taking that nobody caught early enough. Twin studies put addiction heritability at 40 to 70 percent (Verhulst et al., 2015), and the genes driving that do not code for vulnerability to specific things. They code for psychological traits.

Two dopaminergic genes kept coming up. DRD2 Taq1A cuts striatal dopamine receptor density and creates reward insensitivity, which is the well-known pathway to substance addiction (Noble, 2000). COMT Val158Met does something different. The Met allele causes prefrontal dopamine to pile up under stress and that impairs inhibitory control, not reward sensitivity (Kayser et al., 2012). The result is a brain that genuinely cannot stop a dangerous behavior when pressure is highest.

These are different problems with different solutions. DRD2-driven risk needs reward circuit intervention. COMT-driven risk needs stress training and inhibitory control work. Nothing out there separates them. That is the gap I wanted to fill.

03 | Literature Review

The Three Studies That Shaped the Design

1
Kayser et al. (2012)
COMT Val158Met only disrupts inhibitory control when the brain is under cognitive pressure. At baseline the effect basically disappears. That told me I needed to use a stress-load inhibitory control measure as the COMT proxy, not just a general impulse measure.
Design decision: H4MH22 stress-load impulse control used as COMT proxy
2
Stoltenberg et al. (2011)
COMT Met carrier status linked to elevated sensation-seeking in 312 young adults, mediated by impaired behavioral inhibition rather than reward-seeking. Confirms the COMT pathway runs through inhibition, not reward. It must be tested separately from DRD2.
Design decision: COMT and DRD2 tested as competing predictors in the same model
3
Tunbridge et al. (2006)
Estrogen independently upregulates COMT expression in prefrontal cortex, partially buffering the Met allele effect in females. Predicts the COMT pathway is measurably stronger in males. This is a testable sex-specific hypothesis.
Design decision: sex-stratified model trained to test male-specific COMT dominance

Dataset
Add Health Wave IV

6,504 participants, mean age 28.9, free to access through ICPSR Study 21600. The average age being 28.9 when my target population is adolescents is a real limitation I acknowledge. Replication in the ABCD Study (ages 9 to 16, n = 11,878) is the next step.

Model
Gradient Boosting Classifier

I used sklearn GradientBoostingClassifier on the full sample and sex-stratified subgroups. Hyperparameters: 300 estimators max_depth=4 learning_rate=0.05 subsample=0.8. Permutation importance (20 repeats, 5-fold CV) over impurity-based for unbiased rankings.

Outcome Variable
Compulsive Risk-Taking Composite

Binary outcome: above the 85th percentile on H4FS1 across at least two different risk domains in the past year. I chose this threshold because I wanted to capture a consistent pattern of risk behavior, not just one incident.

Methodological Advance
Causal Mediation Analysis

I used causal mediation to split the total COMT effect into two parts: a direct effect (COMT predicting risk-taking on its own) and an indirect effect through inhibitory control failure. If the indirect path carries most of the weight, inhibitory control failure is the causal mechanism. Bootstrap CI (1,000 resamples) on each path coefficient.

What I Found

Plus or minus 0.010 across 5-fold CV. Strong enough to be useful as a real screening tool.

COMT vs DRD2 Importance
COMT-linked inhibitory control failure outranked DRD2 by a factor of 1.8 in males. The gap shrank in females, which is exactly what the estrogen buffering hypothesis predicts.
r=0.58
Feature Importance Correlation
Between male and female feature importance patterns. The two models are genuinely different, not just different in magnitude.
Causal Mediation Finding

Inhibitory control failure carried 67% of the total indirect COMT effect on compulsive risk-taking (95% CI: 54% to 79%, p less than 0.001). This is what makes the GPARI more than a risk score. Knowing inhibitory control failure is the mechanism, not just a correlated feature, is what lets the tool recommend a specific type of intervention rather than just flagging someone as high-risk.

The GPARI Is Live
