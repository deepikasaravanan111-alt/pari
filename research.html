01 | Abstract

Overview

Thousands of adolescents die each year not from substances but from danger itself. This study argues that COMT Val158Met drives prefrontal dopamine accumulation under stress, destroying inhibitory control and producing compulsive risk-taking through a mechanism distinct from DRD2-driven substance addiction. A gradient boosting classifier trained on Add Health Wave IV (n = 6,504) achieves AUC = 0.885. Causal mediation confirms inhibitory control failure carries 67% of the total indirect COMT effect (95% CI: 54% to 79%). The GPARI (Genetic-Psychological Addiction Risk Index) operationalizes this as a deployed open-access tool. Real-time ML inference, personalized risk score, causal pathway label, mediation diagram with bootstrap CI, and matched intervention. Zero cost. Any device. Any school worldwide.
02 | Introduction

Why I Built This

In 2022, unintentional injury was the leading cause of death for Americans aged 1 to 44 (CDC, 2022). Most of those deaths were not random. Twin studies estimate 40 to 70 percent of addiction risk is heritable (Verhulst et al., 2015), and the genes behind that heritability do not encode vulnerability to specific substances. They encode psychological traits.

Two dopaminergic variants matter most here. DRD2 Taq1A reduces striatal dopamine receptor density, producing reward insensitivity that drives substance addiction (Noble, 2000). COMT Val158Met is different. The Met allele causes prefrontal dopamine to build up under stress, which impairs inhibitory control rather than reward sensitivity (Kayser et al., 2012). That is a brain that loses its ability to stop a dangerous behavior when the stakes are highest.

These are two different problems. An adolescent whose risk comes from DRD2 needs reward circuit intervention. One whose risk comes from COMT needs stress-inoculation and inhibitory control training. No existing tool tells you which one you are dealing with. That is what I built the GPARI to do.

03 | Literature Review

Three Findings That Build the Case

1
Kayser et al. (2012)
COMT Val158Met disrupts inhibitory control selectively under cognitive load. The effect is absent at baseline and strongest when prefrontal circuits are taxed. This justifies stress-load inhibitory control as the COMT endophenotype rather than baseline impulse measures.
Design decision: H4MH22 stress-load impulse control used as COMT proxy
2
Stoltenberg et al. (2011)
COMT Met carrier status linked to elevated sensation-seeking in 312 young adults, mediated by impaired behavioral inhibition rather than reward-seeking. Confirms the COMT pathway runs through inhibition, not reward. It must be tested separately from DRD2.
Design decision: COMT and DRD2 tested as competing predictors in the same model
3
Tunbridge et al. (2006)
Estrogen independently upregulates COMT expression in prefrontal cortex, partially buffering the Met allele effect in females. Predicts the COMT pathway is measurably stronger in males. This is a testable sex-specific hypothesis.
Design decision: sex-stratified model trained to test male-specific COMT dominance

Dataset
Add Health Wave IV

6,504 participants (mean age 28.9), freely available through ICPSR Study 21600. Sample age relative to the adolescent target population is a known limitation; ABCD Study replication (ages 9 to 16, n = 11,878) is the primary validation extension.

Model
Gradient Boosting Classifier

sklearn GradientBoostingClassifier trained on full sample and sex-stratified subgroups. Hyperparameters: 300 estimators max_depth=4 learning_rate=0.05 subsample=0.8. Permutation importance (20 repeats, 5-fold CV) over impurity-based for unbiased rankings.

Outcome Variable
Compulsive Risk-Taking Composite

Binary outcome: participants above the 85th percentile on H4FS1 across at least two distinct risk domains in the prior 12 months. Captures behavioral pattern rather than single-incident risk.

Methodological Advance
Causal Mediation Analysis

Decomposes the total COMT effect into a direct path (COMT predicting risk-taking independently) and an indirect path (COMT predicting inhibitory control failure, which predicts risk-taking). If the indirect path dominates, inhibitory control failure is the causal mechanism. Bootstrap CI (1,000 resamples) on each path coefficient.

Causal Mediation Finding

Inhibitory control failure carried 67% of the total indirect COMT effect on compulsive risk-taking (95% CI: 54% to 79%, p less than 0.001). This is not just a predictive relationship. The mediation analysis shows inhibitory control failure is the actual mechanism, not just a correlated feature. That distinction is what makes the intervention recommendation meaningful.
