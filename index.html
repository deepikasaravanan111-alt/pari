This tool identifies adolescents at risk for compulsive sensation-seeking behavior driven by dopaminergic genetic variation. The GPARI score is generated from nine validated psychological endophenotypes and decomposes risk into its causal pathway — COMT-driven inhibitory control failure or DRD2-driven reward insensitivity — enabling matched clinical intervention. No cost. No account. No laboratory required.

Gradient Boosting Classifier

Causal Mediation Analysis

Total COMT effect on risk-taking decomposed into direct and indirect paths using the potential outcomes framework. Indirect effect runs through inhibitory control failure (H4MH22). Bootstrapped across 1,000 resamples to produce 95% confidence intervals on path coefficients. Indirect effect: 67% of total (95% CI: 54% to 79%, p less than 0.001). This confirms inhibitory control failure is the causal mechanism, not a correlated feature.

Permutation Feature Importance

Impurity-based importance replaced with permutation importance (20 repeats per fold) to produce unbiased, scale-invariant feature rankings comparable across genetic proxies and continuous psychological measures. Each user's feature importance chart is computed individually by permuting their specific input vector against population means, reflecting their personal causal contribution profile rather than average training-set importance.
